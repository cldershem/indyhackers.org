    Holiday Social 2012

    What is the best way to celebrate the holidays? Music, food, drink, and friends, old and new!

    Please join us — a bunch of programmers, nerds, geeks, hackers — to celebrate the season with food, drink, music, and friends both old and new. December 15th, 7PM, at The Speak Easy in Broad Ripple, you'll see Pythonistas touching glasses with Java guys, Rubyists joking with the .NET-ers, COBOL gentlemen adjusting their bowler hats — everyone is more than welcome. The best part? It is completely free.

    Update! You can't join us because this event is over. Like, WAY over. And unfortunately, there is no photographic evidence of the 2012 event. But you CAN travel back even further in time and see pictures from the 2011 event, as captured by official Holiday Social documentarian, @thirdshift.

  • What's Happening?

    Last year, we had food, beer, soft drinks, a band, and some prizes. This year is going to be bigger and better.

    • Food

      Last year, the NY Slice food truck brough their portable oven inside to serve up some fresh, piping-hot pizza. Look: I love pizza. However, this year, we're going to switch things up. Details are coming soon, but I am positive you're going to be stoked.

    • Beer

      A keg of local beer kept us all refreshed and happy last year, and this year will be no different. Well, except there will be three of them, from Indianapolis' own Sun King. I told you -- bigger and better.

    • The Handicapper Generals

      The Handicapper Generals kept us warm with plenty of electric blues last year. They'll be back this year, but they're bringing their acoustic instruments, creating a more intimate setting for both music-listening and chatting.

    • Years

      Years, formed in 2008, find themselves on many a highway on their way to the next gig. Recently, they have been blanketing Chicago, including a spot on WGN's national TV broadcast. I am very excited to have them joining us, and bringing some freshly penned holiday songs! All of that, and the bass player — Adam — hacks, and hangs out at Indy.rb on a regular basis.
